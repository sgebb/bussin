Organizing Namespaces: Favorites, Folders & Multi-Tenant

How to bookmark namespaces, organize them into drag-and-drop folders, rename them, and switch between Azure AD tenants - all persisted in your browser.

Favorites

After opening a namespace, you can add it to your Favorites sidebar. Favorited namespaces appear in the left navigation panel for one-click access - no more searching through the full namespace list every time.

All favorites are stored in your browser's local storage. There's no server-side account, so your workspace stays private and works offline.

Folders

As your list of favorites grows, use folders to organize them. Click New Folder at the bottom of the sidebar, give it a name, and start dragging namespaces into it.

Drag and drop

Bussin supports full drag-and-drop for organizing your sidebar:

  • Drag namespaces between folders - move a namespace from one folder to another
  • Reorder namespaces within a folder by dragging them above or below each other
  • Reorder folders - drag folders to rearrange their order
  • Nest folders - drag a folder onto another to create a hierarchy

Every change is saved instantly to local storage.

Rename namespaces

By default, Bussin shows the namespace's Azure resource name. If you work with namespaces like sb-prod-euw-001 and sb-staging-euw-002, you can rename them to friendlier names like "Production Europe" and "Staging Europe". The original hostname is still shown below as a subtitle.

Multi-Tenant Support

Many organizations have multiple Azure AD tenants - for example, a corporate tenant and a client tenant. Bussin's Tenant Selector (in the top bar) lets you switch between all tenants associated with your Microsoft account.

When you switch tenants:

  • Bussin re-authenticates with the new tenant
  • Your namespace list updates to show resources in that directory
  • Your favorites are scoped per tenant - each directory has its own sidebar

This means you can have a completely different workspace for each customer or environment you manage.

Message Statistics

Once inside a namespace, Bussin can pull Azure Monitor metrics directly from the browser. The Stats panel shows:

  • Incoming/Outgoing messages over the last 1, 6, or 24 hours
  • Active messages, Dead-lettered, Scheduled, and Size

This gives you a quick health check without opening the Azure Portal - especially useful when debugging whether messages are being produced or consumed.

(Tip) Dark mode
Click the moon/sun icon in the bottom-left corner to toggle dark mode. Your preference persists across sessions.
